Most early-stage SaaS founders pick usage-based pricing because the big companies use it.

That's the mistake.

Usage-based pricing asks your buyer to predict their own bill before they trust your product. Pre-product-market-fit, they can't. So they hesitate, and your sales cycle gets longer at the exact moment you need it short.

A boring 3-tier seat model wins early. The buyer can approve it without a spreadsheet. You get the deal, then you earn the right to charge for usage later.

Pricing isn't where you signal ambition. It's where you remove friction.

Charge in a way your buyer can say yes to today. Get clever once they're already in.
